## The Space

Welcome to your new favorite coworking space in Madison! Perched on the top floor of a charming vintage building, we’ve got 12-foot ceilings, tons of natural light, and walls bursting with local art. We get it—you didn’t sign up for a boring 9-to-5, so why settle for a stuffy office? Our office space is all about comfort and creativity, with an open layout that sparks cooperation and community, and of course, lightning-fast wifi.

Located one block off the Capitol Square in Madison, Wisconsin, we offer month-to-month all-inclusive memberships. Members choose between hot desks, dedicated desks, and private offices, and all have access to phone booths and reservable meeting rooms.

Need a private space for a quick call? We’ve got you covered with three conference rooms and a couple of cozy phone booths. Feeling podcast-y? Our podcast room/lounge is perfect for watching TV, napping, or recording your next hit show. And don’t worry about running out of steam—our kitchen is always stocked with coffee to keep you buzzing and blazing through your work.

## The Community

When you join the 100state family, you belong to a community of like-minded people who know you by name. Members often develop life-long friendships through shared lunches, ongoing events, and informal gatherings. We fulfill our mission to create a home for problem­ solvers, creatives, and entrepreneurs by providing startups a flexible workspace to host brainstorming sessions, hackathons, and team-building events. We also host community events such as educational talks, creative workshops, networking opportunities, and holiday socials.

## Types of Membership

We offer multiple coworking membership levels to suit various work styles in our on-demand, flexible office space.

#### Day Pass

Use the space for one day during regular business hours (Monday through Friday, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.). Must be booked in advance.

#### Hot Desk

Get 24/7 access to the space and all amenities (printing, conference rooms, kitchen, events etc.). Sit at any available space in the open coworking area.

#### Dedicated Desk

Rent a dedicated desk and get 24/7 access to the shared space and all amenities. Customize your desk with anything that boosts your productivity: monitors, ergonomic aids, personal items, etc.

#### Private Office

Rent a private, lockable office for 1-4 team members to use. Get 24/7 access to the space and all amenities.

about us
100state creates a community and home for problem­ solvers, creatives, and entrepreneurs. We inspire collaboration and create connections to unleash our community’s potential.

### Our Story

100state started with a back of the napkin business plan in 2012. More than a decade later we are still in business and have hosted and grown some of the area’s most successful startups, and provided a friendly and productive workspace for dozens of freelancers, remote workers, artists, and founders.

### Our Mission

100state’s mission is to empower and unite problem solvers, creatives, and entrepreneurs in a collaborative community dedicated to unleashing potential and driving innovation. As a nonprofit organization since June 7, 2013, we are committed to facilitating connections, inspiring collaboration, and creating opportunities through events, brainstorms, and an inspiring environment that addresses our community’s most pressing challenges

## Our Vision

#### We envision a community of change agents working together to create a new model of innovation that helps the world navigate the evolving human experience of the 21st century

## The creative arm of 100state

100arts represents the creative branch of 100state. As part of an organization that champions the intersection between art, education and entrepreneurship, we push to create space for artists in the professional world. Artists, like other professionals, are freelancers, small business owners, educators, mentors and organizers. All professionals need access to resources and we believe that an emphasis on community building builds for better businesses. Come visit the space to see our latest installation and keep an eye out for our newsletter for upcoming events and important announcements!

Growing up in southern Wisconsin, Mushka was fortunate to have friends and family support his journey in the Arts. After having transplanted himself to the dreamy Caribbean, Boston and New York, he is now back home in Madison, where he volunteers for the MMoCA and continues Life Drawing investigations on the UW Campus. ### History

**Why is the name 100state when you’re not actually at 100state Street?**

100State was the original address and location above Ian’s Pizza on State Street when 100state started back in 2013.

**Is 100state a for-profit or a non-profit organization?**

100state is setup as a 501 (c) 3 non-profit organization.

**How many locations has 100state had?**

Four (4). 100 State Street (4th floor above Ian’s), 30 West Mifflin St (on the capital square above Coopers), 316 West Washington Avenue, and our current location, 17 South Fairchild Street.

### Utilities

**Where are the paper towels and toilet paper?**

* Toiletry items including soap, should we ever run out, can be found next to the fridge under the red cabinet. There may also be a stash under the counter in the respective bathrooms. These are on auto-replenish from Amazon and scheduled to replenish every month.

**Coffee machines?**

1. Where are grounds kept? In the cabinet on the far left in the kitchen.
2. How to order more coffee? 100state does not have an ongoing coffee service. Coffee grounds are provided by the organization through partnerships with local roasters and members who want to bring in their own coffee. If you would like to provide a month of coffee for 100state, please email hello@100state.com subject line: Coffee

**Where are the lights for the building?**

* Most lights are on the walls near the doors. The two sets of master lights are to the left of the elevator and to the right of the phone booth closest to the Pressure Cooker (office room).

**How to change temperature?**

1. There are two thermostats on the floor
2. One is in the Government Oversight room, the other is on the wall near the printers. Please do not change the thermostats without contacting the others near you in the space or the 100state staff.

### Parking

For full day visits, we recommend that you park in the ramp on the corner of Fairchild and East Main Street.

For shorter visits, there are 25 minute, 1 hour and 2 hour parking meters in front of the building on Fairchild St and along East Main Street and East Henry Street.

Meters are enforced 8am-6pm Monday through Saturday.

**How can I change or make payment arrangements?**

* Payments are handled through Moonclerk and Stripe. Please email hello@100state.com if you have questions or concerns about membership payments. Alternatively there is a link at the bottom of your monthly email receipt where you can update your payment information.

**Can I bring a non-member?**

* Absolutely, we encourage you to bring friends and co-workers from time to time. If someone wants to become a regular user of the space, we would encourage them to pursue regular membership.

**Who do I contact if I have an issue?**

* The quickest way to get a response is to ping the #general channel in Slack. Slack is a great app to have open throughout the day to keep in touch with coworkers and abreast of things going on in the space (treats, events, questions, games….etc)

* Ping the Slack channel as a first step. Also a good idea to get a cell phone number from a coworker or two just in case. Joseph can be reached at his cell phone # above.

**How do I reserve a conference room or check if they’re free?**

#### What is the Founder’s Circle Ignite Program?

The Founder’s Circle Ignite program is a dynamic initiative aimed at equipping aspiring entrepreneurs with the tools, resources, and mentorship they need to turn their ideas into thriving businesses. This innovative collaborative initiative between 100state and NHTI, transforms local entrepreneurs’ dreams into thriving businesses, strengthening Madison’s growing startup ecosystem.

The program focuses on fostering innovation, building community connections, and providing hands-on learning experiences. Participants gain access to workshops, networking opportunities, and expert guidance, all tailored to help them navigate the challenges of starting and scaling a business. This year’s cohort included a diverse group of entrepreneurs, each bringing unique ideas and perspectives to the table.  Among the graduates were individuals like **Adam Jun**, **Kiara Allen**, **James Gui**, and **Dr. Giselle Argento-Berrios**, who represent the next wave of innovative thinkers and business leaders in Madison.

# Is Paying for a Coworking Space Worth It? A Guide for Remote Workers, Freelancers, and Consultants

![Coworking space with open tables and natural light](data:image/svg+xml;base64,PHN2ZyB4bWxucz0iaHR0cDovL3d3dy53My5vcmcvMjAwMC9zdmciIHdpZHRoPSIxMjAwIiBoZWlnaHQ9IjgwMCIgdmlld0JveD0iMCAwIDEyMDAgODAwIj48cmVjdCB3aWR0aD0iMTAwJSIgaGVpZ2h0PSIxMDAlIiBmaWxsPSIjY2ZkNGRiIi8+PC9zdmc+)

Posted on November 4, 2024

As remote work has taken off since the pandemic, many professionals in various fields find themselves wondering: are coworking spaces worth the cost?   For those tired of working from home or feeling limited in coffee shops, coworking spaces offer a tempting alternative.

This article examines the benefits and potential downsides of coworking spaces compared to working from home or a coffee shop to help you decide if it’s worth the investment.

## The Benefits of Coworking Spaces

Working from home offers convenience and cost-effectiveness, but coworking spaces provide a sense of community and a more structured work environment that many find beneficial.

1. **Community and** **Networking Opportunities**Many remote workers and digital nomads find a sense of community lacking when they’re isolated in their home offices. Coworking spaces bring together a mix of professionals, from freelancers to entrepreneurs, allowing for organic networking opportunities. Some coworking memberships even include exclusive networking events, fostering relationships that can lead to partnerships or collaborations across various fields.
2. **Improved Mental Health and** **Work-Life Balance**Working from home can blur the line between personal and professional life, affecting one’s work-life balance. Having a separate office environment helps create mental separation, which is beneficial for well-being. A daily commute—even if it’s just a short one to the coworking space—provides time to mentally shift between personal and professional roles. Many find that establishing a regular routine helps them become more productive and happier with their work environment.
3. **Physical Health Benefits**It’s easy to stay stationary at home, but a coworking space encourages movement. The commute itself gets you up and active, and some coworking spaces even offer access to gyms or standing desks. Additionally, coworking spaces often have ergonomic office equipment, which can prevent the physical strain that sometimes comes with a home office setup.
4. ******Educational Workshops and Resources******Coworking spaces often host educational workshops or offer access to resources like office supplies and high-speed internet. These events are often included in the membership fee and can provide valuable learning opportunities without additional cost.
5. **Meeting Rooms** ******for Professional Gatherings******Meeting clients at a coffee shop may work in some situations, but coworking spaces offer the privacy and professionalism of reservable meeting rooms. And even if you have a cushy home office setup, you might not have a clean and professional space in your home to meet clients. Conference rooms in coworking spaces create a comfortable, client-ready environment that doesn’t require sharing your home address.

## OK, But What About Coffee Shops?

While coffee shops offer a change of scenery, coworking spaces are specifically designed for productivity and connection.

1. **Reliable** **Wi-Fi** **and** **Phone Booths**One drawback of working at a coffee shop is the potential for unstable internet connections — or WiFi policies that kick you off after an hour or two. Coworking spaces, however, provide reliable high-speed internet, allowing you to work without interruptions. Additionally, coworking spaces often include phone booths or private rooms, giving you a place to take calls without disrupting others.
2. **Cost Savings on Food and Drink**Working at a coffee shop is not as cost-effective as it may seem if you want to log long workdays away from home. Most coffee shops require you to make a purchase at least every two hours to justify your presence. Coworking spaces allow you to bring your own lunch, which can save money over time. Some spaces even offer coffee or snacks as part of the membership, which can help offset the cost of membership.
3. **Focus and Productivity**Coworking spaces offer an environment dedicated to work, helping you stay focused for longer stretches. Many remote workers find it easier to dive into complex projects in a coworking space than in a bustling coffee shop. The design of coworking spaces, with options like hot desks or dedicated desks, supports varied working styles.
4. ****Building Relationships with Other Professionals****Unlike the transient environment of a coffee shop, coworking spaces offer a collaborative environment that promotes relationship-building. The connections you make in a coworking space can evolve into a support network that’s valuable, whether you’re a remote worker or managing your own startup business.

## Downsides of Coworking Spaces Compared to Working from Home

Despite the benefits, coworking spaces aren’t for everyone. Here are a few potential drawbacks to consider.

1. **Membership Fees** **and** **Pricing**Coworking membership does come at a cost, and not all professionals can justify the expense. Memberships at coworking spaces in Madison, Wisconsin, cost $150 per month on average for a hot desk, with some spaces charging much higher rates for private spaces. However, many companies provide reimbursement for coworking fees, making it easier for remote workers to cover membership costs.
2. **Commuting Time**For those who cherish the lack of a commute, adding travel time to a coworking space can feel like a drawback. However, the commute can add a sense of routine and structure to your workday, which many find beneficial.
3. **Open** ****Office Environment****Coworking spaces often have an open layout, which can be challenging for those who don’t thrive in shared spaces. That said, many coworking spaces offer a mix of shared workspace and private offices, allowing you to choose an environment that suits your working style and budget.

## Conclusion: Are Coworking Spaces Worth It?

Whether you’re a freelancer, a business owner, or a remote worker, coworking spaces offer a unique work environment that can balance productivity and social connection. With high-speed Wi-Fi, meeting rooms, phone booths, and networking events, coworking spaces provide amenities that both enhance professional success and support mental well-being. While working from home and coffee shops have their own appeal, a shared office space might be the key to a more fulfilling remote work experience—an investment that can improve both your work and personal life.

We hope this discussion of the pros and cons of coworking spaces helps you make a choice that aligns with your needs, work style, and budget. If you are someone who values structure, community, and a dedicated office environment, we’d love to have you join us at 100state! Book a tour today.

# 3 Reasons Why I Love Coworking as a Digital Nomad and Solopreneur

![community-support](data:image/svg+xml;base64,PHN2ZyB4bWxucz0iaHR0cDovL3d3dy53My5vcmcvMjAwMC9zdmciIHdpZHRoPSIxNDAwIiBoZWlnaHQ9IjEwNTAiIHZpZXdCb3g9IjAgMCAxNDAwIDEwNTAiPjxyZWN0IHdpZHRoPSIxMDAlIiBoZWlnaHQ9IjEwMCUiIGZpbGw9IiNjZmQ0ZGIiLz48L3N2Zz4=)

Posted on March 21, 2024

*The following blog post was written by long-time 100state member and solopreneur, Olivia Barrow.*

![Solopreneur Olivia Borrow](data:image/svg+xml;base64,PHN2ZyB4bWxucz0iaHR0cDovL3d3dy53My5vcmcvMjAwMC9zdmciIHdpZHRoPSIzMDAiIGhlaWdodD0iMzAwIiB2aWV3Qm94PSIwIDAgMzAwIDMwMCI+PHJlY3Qgd2lkdGg9IjEwMCUiIGhlaWdodD0iMTAwJSIgZmlsbD0iI2NmZDRkYiIvPjwvc3ZnPg==)When I quit my job in 2016 to launch my SEO strategy and content creation business, I was excited to become a solopreneur digital nomad, meaning I could perform my job from anywhere. For the first nine months, I did just that. I bounced around between a dozen different coffee shops in Madison, my partner’s apartment, my parents’ house in North Carolina, and the Red River Gorge in Kentucky, until I decided to really embrace the digital nomad stereotype and bought a ticket to Bangkok. I spent nine weeks backpacking around South East Asia, fitting my work in between rock climbing days and sightseeing days.

While I had a wonderful time in Thailand, Vietnam, and Cambodia and will never forget the experiences I had there, when I got home from the trip, I was desperately craving something that I’d had a hard time finding ever since I quit my job: community.

Before the jet lag even wore off, I started touring coworking spaces in Madison, looking not just for a quiet space to focus but a place where I would feel grounded in routine and community. I wanted a place to belong. After evaluating five or six different options around the city, I landed at 100state, and I’ve been here ever since.

100state stands out in the Madison coworking scene for several reasons. As one of the only nonprofit coworking spaces in the area, the rates are very affordable. Secondly, it’s an active art gallery, with a rotating selection of art from dozens of Madison-based artists hanging on the walls year round. It adds so much color to the space, and when I’m in need of inspiration, sometimes I gaze at one of my favorite paintings and let my mind wander. But in my opinion, the most important way 100state stands out is the community. The community at 100state is one of the most vibrant I’ve ever experienced in a coworking space (and I’ve been a member at two others during some other extended periods of travel). At 100state, the regulars know each other by name. Our lunch room is one of the first things you see off the elevator, which makes it a natural gathering place. We chat, share food and snacks, and recently, thanks to community manager Kelly Pagac, we’ve even been practicing yoga and mediation together.

But the community isn’t the only thing I love about 100state and about coworking in general. I’ve broken it down into three reasons I love coworking.

**1. Networking with Other Business Owners**

As a solopreneur business owner, maintaining a membership at 100state has provided excellent networking opportunities, even when I wasn’t looking for them. I’m an extrovert, so I’m always making an effort to get to know other members, whether they are remote workers, artists, grad students, interns, or entrepreneurs like me. And throughout the years, these efforts have frequently turned into opportunities for my business. The affordable month-to-month membership fee attracts many entrepreneurs who are still in the early stages of their business, and through the years I’ve helped a few business owners create foundational content for their websites. I’ve also gotten excellent referrals to some long-term clients from connections I made over the lunch table at 100state.

**2. Personal and Professional Development Opportunities**

100state has always been a place for knowledge sharing. In the early years, it was known for hosting community-wide brainstorming and problem-solving sessions. Through the years the types of events have changed, but there have been dozens of free educational events, workshops, panels about topics ranging from how to get a business loan and other challenges entrepreneurs face to workshops about mindfulness, creativity, and personal finance. One cool knowledge-sharing opportunity that Kelly recently launched is what we’re calling “AV Club,” a support group and cheering squad for anybody who wants to dabble in short-form video marketing. Before the club launched, I hadn’t been seriously thinking about doing any video marketing for my business. But now, knowing I’ll have peers to bounce ideas off of, learn logistical tips and tricks from, and get feedback from, I’m excited to dive into a project that might just be transformative for my business.

**3. A Home Base in Downtown Madison**

I love that 100state is located in the heart of downtown Madison. I live a few miles east of downtown, so it takes me about 20 minutes to commute to the office. In the winter, I enjoy reading a book on my quiet commute on the bus, and when the weather warms up, I ride my bike for more flexibility (although, if I’m honest, I miss the built-in reading time on the bus!). But whether I’m biking or busing, the commute helps me mentally and emotionally separate my work life from my personal life.

We have a secure indoor bike garage on the first floor, which makes it really easy to bike in. And on days where big events are happening downtown, like Concerts on the Square, it’s a big perk to not fight for a bike parking spot (much less a car parking spot!). If I want to go for a bike ride on a nice evening after work, I feel comfortable leaving my stuff for a few hours in the office and using my 24/7 keycard to get back in later — so I can maximize the daylight hours instead of losing time commuting.

If you’re a soloentrepreneur craving community, routine, and a quiet place free from the distractions that can arise at home, check out a membership at 100state!

# Key Benefits of Consulting a Mentor for Your Business Idea

![entrepreneur-side-hustle](data:image/svg+xml;base64,PHN2ZyB4bWxucz0iaHR0cDovL3d3dy53My5vcmcvMjAwMC9zdmciIHdpZHRoPSIxMjAwIiBoZWlnaHQ9IjkwMCIgdmlld0JveD0iMCAwIDEyMDAgOTAwIj48cmVjdCB3aWR0aD0iMTAwJSIgaGVpZ2h0PSIxMDAlIiBmaWxsPSIjY2ZkNGRiIi8+PC9zdmc+)

Posted on January 22, 2024

Embarking on an entrepreneurial journey can be thrilling yet daunting. One crucial step often overlooked by many aspiring entrepreneurs is the invaluable role of a mentor. When you have a business idea, consulting with a mentor can be the difference between a successful launch and a missed opportunity. This article delves into the myriad benefits of mentorship in business and entrepreneurship.

Let’s jump in!

##### Understanding the Concept of Business Mentorship

Business mentorship is an arrangement where an experienced professional provides guidance, knowledge, and advice to someone just starting or looking to grow in business. Unlike advisors or coaches, mentors offer more personalized and long-term support, focusing on overall development rather than just specific issues.

##### The Importance of Mentorship in Early Business Stages

In the initial stages of a business, the path is fraught with challenges and unknowns. With their experience and expertise, a mentor can help navigate these rough waters, offering advice tailored to the unique challenges of startups.

##### Mentorship and Business Strategy Development

Strategizing is crucial in business, and a mentor can play a pivotal role in refining your business ideas. They bring a fresh perspective, often backed by their own successes and failures, to help you develop a robust business strategy.

##### Networking Opportunities through Mentorship

A significant advantage of having a mentor is access to their network. A well-connected mentor can open doors to partnerships, resources, and opportunities otherwise inaccessible to budding entrepreneurs.

##### Learning from Experience: Gaining Insights from a Mentor’s Journey

The journey of a mentor is a goldmine of lessons and insights. By sharing their experiences, mentors provide a real-life context to business challenges, offering a more practical understanding than any textbook.

##### Avoiding Common Pitfalls: How a Mentor Can Help

Business is often a trial-and-error process, but a mentor can help in steering clear of common mistakes. Learning from their experiences can save you from costly missteps.

##### Mentors as a Source of Motivation and Support

Starting a business can be a lonely journey. A mentor can offer much-needed emotional support and motivation, especially during tough times, helping you stay focused and resilient.

##### Customized Guidance: Tailoring Advice to Your Specific Business Idea

Each business idea is unique, and so should be the advice you receive. A mentor provides tailored guidance, ensuring that the advice is relevant to your specific business model and industry.

##### Critical Feedback and Its Importance

Constructive criticism is vital for growth. Mentors provide honest feedback, helping you to develop resilience and learn from your weaknesses.

##### Mentorship in Enhancing Leadership Skills

Effective leadership is crucial for business success. Mentorship can play a significant role in honing your leadership skills, preparing you to manage teams and lead your business effectively.

##### Financial Guidance and Resource Management

Financial mismanagement can quickly lead to business failure. A mentor with expertise in finance can guide you in resource allocation, budgeting, and financial planning.

##### Innovating and Pivoting with Mentor Input

The ability to innovate and pivot is essential in today’s fast-paced business world. A mentor can provide critical insights and guidance in adapting and evolving your business model to stay competitive.

##### Building a Strong Business Foundation with Mentorship

A mentor helps lay a solid foundation for your business, emphasizing sustainability and long-term success over quick, short-term gains.

##### Global Perspectives: Learning from a Mentor with Diverse Experience

A mentor with a diverse background can provide a broader perspective, helping you think globally and expanding the scope of your business idea.

##### Mentorship and Personal Growth

Mentorship is not just about business growth but personal development, too. It helps balance personal and professional life, leading to overall growth and fulfillment.

##### How to Find and Choose the Right Mentor for Your Business Idea

Selecting the right mentor is critical. Look for someone with relevant experience, a network beneficial to your field, and a mentoring style that matches your learning preferences.

The benefits of consulting a mentor when you have a business idea are manifold. From strategic insights to emotional support, a mentor can guide you through the complexities of entrepreneurship, setting you on the path to success.

https://www.instagram.com/senoritagalleta/

Angelica Contreras is a Latinx artist who explores the multi-layered relationships between identity, tradition, and popular culture. Growing up in the US and Mexico provided a rich palette of imagery for Contreras. Within her artwork, there is an element of nostalgia present in its layers; sometimes reminiscent of childhood, adolescence or tying in specific cultural references and traditions. Contreras incorporates the mediums of acrylic and oil painting and techniques of collage into her artwork, creating a rich palette of colors and textures. Her use of materials is as calculated as it is serendipitous, resulting in unusual cultural combinations and visual compositions. Her work acts as a bridge between various communities and cultures while reflecting her own life experiences.

Contreras was born in Whittier, California and moved to Guadalajara, Jalisco at an early age. She attended the University of Guadalajara, receiving a B.A. in art in 2008 and a Masters in Art Education in 2016. Her artwork has been shown in exhibitions in Mexico and the U.S., including “Caja de Visiones” at Madison Museum of Contemporary Art and “The Hidden Faces/ Los Rostros Ocultos” at Latino Arts Inc in Milwaukee. Contreras was a recipient of the Forward Art Prize in 2020, an annual award targeted to support the work of female artists in Dane County. She currently lives and works in Madison, Wisconsin, where she continues to create and showcase her art.

## Ann Haberl

https://annhaberl.com

https://www.instagram.com/meditativelandscapes/

There is a stillness in my work. It’s the way you feel when time seems to stop – at birth, death, and if you’re lucky during some awestruck moment in your life. Paradoxically, it is also the way you feel when time is racing by – clouds casting complex shadows on a lake, the changing sky at dusk. I’m holding that note in my paintings. The content of my work is about the transitory nature of images, indeed of life. The light is moving, and in a breath it will be gone. I paint to catch that moment, the moment before the light goes. As in meditation, there is no clear destination here. You will not end up at the little red barn. There is mystery and permission to wander. The narrative, if any, is ethereal. A subtle supernatural quality touches all of my work. There is a presence and a vastness. I don’t dictate the presence, I just invite it. The invitation sometimes starts with a respectful disregard for the classic tradition of atmospheric perspective. The juxtaposition of a loose painterly foreground – often pushed to abstraction – and a tighter, focused background or middle ground contribute to a slightly off kilter feeling. The viewer is just a little uncomfortable in this soothing space, and being out of the comfort zone forces one to pay attention. Like a mental float tank the experience is one of relaxed alertness. In that dichotomy – the beautiful and the edgy – is the presence I beckon.

## Annemarie Adams

https://www.annemarieadamsgallery.com

To me the physical world is a veil that falls in front of the energetic world. And the energetic world plays with us constantly. I love to watch and listen for the signs it sends us.

When I take photographs I look for an essence that can be brought forward: a set of colors, a pattern, a texture, a contrast. I see that essence as a piece of art that can be made out of the composition of the photo. Sometimes the details are essential and at other times a distraction. I use different artistic styles to bring out the essence I see.

I’ve had a lifelong love of photography that includes professional work directing commercial photo shoots.

Giving back

It’s been a dream of mine to support music educators.

My Italian ancestors were classically trained musicians. I started learning Spanish classical guitar at age 8 and in high school, band was my refuge. In mid-life, I took classical voice lessons and was later accepted into the Madison Symphony Chorus, where I’ve been singing since 2014.

I wear another hat in the music world, as host of a morning radio show. On-air I’m Ava Borelli, host of Ava Borelli’s Classical Jukebox on WORT 89.9 FM (wortfm.org) our local volunteer-powered community radio station. Together with my co-host Walter, we are on WORT FM each week combining classcial pops, film scores and mashups with banter, musician interviews, entertainment news and music history. None of this would have been possible without my music teachers.

To pay it forward, a portion of proceeds go to the Madison Youth Choir and the Save the Music Foundation.

My sense of affinity for The Big Easy runs deep, so a portion of the proceeds from the sale of New Orleans art goes to Youth Rebuilding New Orleans.

Thank you for supporting my small business and being part of this journey to bring music and purpose into the lives of many.
