## Transcend UW History

## 2013

1ST ANNUAL STARTUP CHALLENGE

In 2013, Transcend UW hosted its first annual startup challenge, which brought together students from a variety of disciplines to pitch their ideas and compete for funding. The event was a huge success and has since become an annual tradition.

## 2010

TRANSCEND UW IS FOUNDED

Transcend UW was founded in 2010 with the goal of fostering a culture of innovation and entrepreneurship on campus. Our first event was a small workshop on idea generation, and since then we have grown to become one of the largest and most active student organizations on campus.

# MISSION STATEMENT

##### Transcend UW is a student organization focused on providing invaluable resources and opportunities to students on campus who are interested in creating their own ventures. We do not limit our member’s ideas to tech, mobile applications or purely entrepreneurial ventures; instead we encourage collaboration between majors and focuses which we hope inspires start-up ideas that can change the world.

##### ​

##### Our utility comes from the vast network of alumni and professionals from the Madison area and beyond which we tap regularly for inspiring talks and informative workshops that our members can utilize to expand their knowledge beyond their curriculum and hopefully apply it towards innovation. Our impact goes beyond the resources we provide and the knowledge we impart to our members.

##### Our core concept is to inspire the Madison student community to create businesses and innovative ideas that will change the world.

## Competition Structure

### Location (Both Days): Discovery Building 330 N Orchard Street, Madison, WI 53715

### ​

#### Day 1 – April 16

* Pitches run from 9:00 AM – 5:30 PM
* You will receive a SignupGenius to select your pitch slot

Pitch Format:

* Total time: 10 minutes (hard stop)
* Recommended: 7 min pitch + 3 min Q&A
* No live demos allowed

  + You may include images or pre-recorded videos in your presentation

Poster Session (12:00 – 1:00 PM):

* Open to the public
* Think of this as a chance to showcase your product more interactively
* Teams who opt in via the deliverables form will be assigned a table
* Live demos ARE allowed here
* Judges will walk around during this time

Results:

* Finalist decisions will be sent by 10:00 PM
* Selected teams will receive a follow-up + SignupGenius for Day 2 slots

#### Day 2 – April 17

* Top 16 teams from Day 1 will advance

Format Changes (Important):

* Pitches start at 8:20 AM
* Pitch: 10 minutes (hard stop)
* Followed by: 10 minutes of Q&A
* Live demos ARE allowed as part of your pitch
* All final pitches will be open to the public and take place on the main stage (anyone can be in the audience)
* Pitches conclude at 4:00 PM sharp
* Followed by:

  + Networking + Awards Ceremony (4:00 – 6:00 PM)

​

We would highly recommend at least one person from your team

being present for the networking and awards. Failure to have a team member

present will result in forfeiting your award (i.e., if your team wins but no one is there, the award will not be given).

## Prizes

### ​All teams from Day 1 are eligible for a $2,500 prize

​​

* Additional prizes will be distributed among finalists
* Full breakdown will be made public one week before the competition

​

## What Judges Are Looking For

Here are some of the key things judges will be looking for:

​

* Team – Why you? What gives you an edge to build this?
* Market – Real problem, real demand, meaningful size
* Traction as a business – Users, revenue, pilots, validation
* Overall growth potential – Can this actually scale into something big?

​

Note: This is a pitch competition, but pitching well alone won’t carry you. Judges care about whether you’re building something real.
